## Overview

The **Gambling Regulation Act 2024** (No. 35 of 2024) is the primary legislation governing gambling in Ireland. It established the Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland (GRAI) as the national regulator for gambling activities.

All providers of gambling activities operating in Ireland, or providing gambling products/services to consumers in Ireland, require a gambling licence granted by the GRAI.

**GRAI was established on 5 March 2025.**

The associated **Gambling (Licence Application Fees) Regulations 2025** set out the tiered fee structure for licence applications.

---

## Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland (GRAI)

**Full name:** Údarás Rialála Cearrbhachais na hÉireann  
**Website:** [www.grai.ie](https://www.grai.ie)  
**Address:** Ballaugh House, 73–79 Mount Street Lower, Dublin 2  
**Contact:** [www.grai.ie/contact-us](https://www.grai.ie/contact-us)

### Functions of the GRAI

- Licensing and regulating gambling providers in Ireland
- Establishing and maintaining the National Gambling Exclusion Register (NER)
- Administering the Social Impact Fund
- Setting standards for gambling products and services
- Enforcement of relevant obligations
- Publishing Codes of Practice

### Funding

The GRAI is funded through fees charged to licence applicants and an annual charge imposed on licensees to fund operational costs (s.41). Licensees must pay this charge within 30 days of notice.

## Licence Types

### Business-to-Consumer (B2C) Licences

| Licence Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **In-person Betting Licence** | Authorises provision of betting activities from one or more premises in Ireland, or pool betting for an event or series of events |
| **Remote Betting Licence** | Authorises provision of betting activities by remote means (including pool betting by remote means) |
| **Remote Betting Intermediary Licence** | Authorises provision of a facility enabling individuals to bet with other individuals (not the intermediary) by remote means |
| **In-person Gaming Licence** | Authorises provision of gaming activities from premises in Ireland |
| **Remote Gaming Licence** | Authorises provision of gaming activities by remote means |
| **In-person Lottery Licence** | Authorises provision of lottery activities from premises in Ireland |
| **Remote Lottery Licence** | Authorises provision of lottery activities by remote means |
| **Gambling Licence for Charitable or Philanthropic Purpose** | For charitable/philanthropic gambling activities |

### Business-to-Business (B2B) Licences

Licences for providers of gambling products and gambling-related services to other licensees. Applications will be accepted at a later stage (phased rollout).

### Phased Opening for Licence Applications

The GRAI is adopting a phased approach. Betting licence applications were the initial focus. Gaming and lottery licence applications will follow. B2B licence applications will be accepted at a later stage.

> In the interim, existing operators are encouraged to apply for and renew licensing under existing arrangements with the Revenue Commissioners under the Betting Act 1931 and the Gaming and Lotteries Act 1956. Revenue Commissioners will continue issuing betting licences in 2025 for a one-year term only to facilitate transition.

## Licensing Exemptions

Certain lotteries are exempt from licensing requirements, including:

- Certain lotteries held for charitable or philanthropic purposes (s.92)
- Certain lotteries held in conjunction with the selling or marketing of products (s.93)

Full exemption criteria are available on the GRAI website.

## Application Process

### Step 1 — Publish Notice of Intention

Publish a Notice of Intention to apply for a gambling licence **at least 28 days before** submitting your application. Provide the GRAI with a copy (including proof of publication).

### Step 2 — Prepare Documentation

All documentation must be accurate and truthful. Required documents include:

**Section 1 — Business Registration**

- Trading name, registered business address, tax clearance information
- Certificate of Incorporation, Constitution documents, Shareholders Register
- Anti-Money Laundering / Counter-Terrorism Financing policy
- Know Your Customer (KYC) policy
- Responsible Gambling policy
- Risk Management policy
- Whistleblower Policy
- Business Continuity Plans
- Financial Statements, Bank Statements, Loan Agreements
- Business Plan with Financial Projections
- Details of any other gambling or non-gambling licences held or previously held (Ireland or overseas)
- Details of beneficial owners and relevant officers
- Details of any convictions, criminal proceedings or regulatory action against the applicant, its officers or beneficial owners

**Section 1b — Account Officer Registration (Corporate Bodies)**

Each beneficial owner and relevant officer must provide:
- Proof of identity and address
- Details of any gambling or non-gambling licences held/previously held
- Details of any criminal convictions for a relevant offence
- Tax clearance information

**Section 2 — Licence Application**

Confirm the licence type and, depending on type, provide:
- In-person betting channel(s) (on course, off course, or both)
- Event details (for pool betting)
- Premises details (locations, maps, floor plans, deeds/lease, children permitted, ATM provision)
- Technical and software information (for remote gambling)
- Customer terms and conditions

### Step 3 — Submit Application and Pay Fee

Complete the application online. The full, non-refundable application fee must be paid at time of submission.

### Step 4 — Review

The GRAI performs a risk-based assessment of the applicant's suitability. Additional information may be requested. The GRAI may request or exchange information with other regulatory bodies as part of due diligence.

### Step 5 — Premises Assessment (In-person Licences)

A GRAI representative will physically attend the premises to confirm suitability. Supporting documentation required includes maps, Planning Permission, Certificates of Compliance with Building Regulations, Planning and Fire Safety documentation.

### Step 6 — Decision

Applicants are notified in writing on the decision to grant or refuse a licence.

### Step 7 — Post-Licence Obligations

Licensees must notify the GRAI of any material changes, including:
- Changes in ownership or control
- Changes in financial circumstances (may require a variation of licence if likely to exceed the fee category limit)
- Conviction of a relevant officer, beneficial owner or licensee for a relevant offence

## Key Obligations for B2C Licensees

*Source: Guidance on Relevant Obligations for Business to Consumer Licensees, GRAI (17 February 2026)*

The following obligations apply to B2C licensees. Breaches may result in criminal prosecution or enforcement action.

---

### Licence Display (s.110)

- **In-person licensees** must display their licence in a prominent location at each premises.
- **Remote licensees** must display their licence in a prominent location on all online platforms (websites, apps).
- The licence registration number must be included in all business correspondence and advertisements relating to gambling activities.
- Licensees must provide a copy of their licence to GRAI authorised officers on request.

---

### Licence Conditions — General (s.131)

B2C gambling licences are subject to the following conditions:

- Having the financial capacity to fund the relevant gambling activity and winnings
- Funding winnings from lawful activities
- The licensee, relevant officers and beneficial owners continuing to be fit and proper persons
- Providing only the gambling activity authorised under the licence
- Payments and winnings not exceeding maximum limits (Schedule 3 of the Act)
- Displaying maximum payment/winnings limits prominently at each premises and online platform
- Notifying the GRAI of changes to information given in licence application (within 7 days)
- Paying the annual charge to fund GRAI operational costs within 30 days of notice

---

### Segregated Customer Account (s.135)

Licensees must open and maintain a **single Segregated Customer Account** with a regulated financial service provider to hold customer funds.

The account must hold:
- Money held by account-holders and in-person participants
- Payments made in connection with gambling activities not yet completed
- Unpaid winnings

Licensees may only access these funds when:
- Given permission by the account-holder or participant
- The gambling activity has been completed
- To pay out winnings

Licensees must maintain up-to-date records identifying:
- The amount standing to the credit of each account-holder/participant
- The total amount of payments held
- The amount of unpaid winnings

**Criminal offence to fail to comply.**

---

### Notifying GRAI of Material Changes (s.153)

Licensees must notify the GRAI in writing **within 7 days** of any of the following **material events**:

1. A change in relevant officers or beneficial owners
2. A change to the licensee's address for receipt of notices
3. Conviction of the licensee, a relevant officer or beneficial owner for a Schedule 1 offence, or proceedings commenced for such an offence
4. A finding or sanction by a regulatory authority, court or tribunal outside Ireland relating to gambling obligations
5. A change in the licensee's financial circumstances that impacts their capacity to fund winnings or comply with licence conditions

---

### Payment of Winnings (s.155)

Licensees must not unreasonably withhold payment of winnings.

Winnings may reasonably be withheld where:
- The licensee becomes aware of a suspicious gambling pattern in relation to the activity
- The participant has breached reasonable contractual terms and conditions

---

### Suspicious Gambling Patterns (s.156)

If a licensee becomes aware of a suspicious gambling pattern (i.e. an attempt to influence the outcome of a gambling activity), they must:

1. **Stop accepting payments** in relation to that gambling activity
2. **Issue written notice** to each person attempting to make a payment, stating no further payments will be accepted
3. **Notify the GRAI as soon as possible** of the suspicious pattern
4. **Provide copies** of all notices and records to the GRAI
5. **Inform An Garda Síochána** as soon as possible
6. **Withhold winnings** until the GRAI confirms they may be paid out

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply with items 1, 2, 3, 5, or 6.

Examples of suspicious gambling patterns:
- Bets placed on events where integrity is later questioned by an official body
- Bets placed by individuals who could influence the outcome (e.g. a participant betting on their own competition)
- A large and sudden volume of bets on a particular outcome, especially in low-volume markets

---

### Inducements (s.157)

Inducements (e.g. free bets, money-back guarantees) may only be offered to the **general public**. They **cannot** be offered to:
- Specific individuals
- Specific groups of persons
- Customers selected based on loyalty or betting volume

Reward or loyalty schemes for customers based on betting volume are prohibited.

**Criminal offence** to breach these restrictions.

---

### Children — Employment and Participation (s.142, s.158)

- Licensees must **not employ or engage a child** (under 18) to provide gambling activities. **(s.142)**
- Licensees must **not permit a child to participate** in gambling activities. **(s.158)**
- If there is doubt about a participant's age, appropriate photo ID (e.g. passport, driving licence) must be sought before allowing participation.
- For remote licensees, all participants must open an account and provide appropriate identity documentation.
- Reasonable mistake as to age may be a defence, provided the licensee took appropriate steps.

---

### Training (s.163)

Licensees must ensure that all staff whose duties entail engaging with participants have completed a **GRAI-approved training programme** before commencing work and at intervals required by the GRAI.

The GRAI publishes approved training programmes and standards on its website.

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

---

### Spending Limits (s.164)

Licensees must enable participants to set a **spending limit** for gambling, covering:

- The amount they can pay for any individual gambling activity
- The total amount they can pay across all gambling activities provided by the licensee

Participants must be able to set the time period for their spending limit. There are no restrictions on how long the period can be. Once set, participants **cannot remove or increase** the limit until after the time period expires.

Licensees must not:
- Provide a gambling activity that would cause the participant to exceed their limit
- Communicate with the participant to invite participation in gambling while the limit applies
- If the limit is exceeded, the licensee must **refund the excess within 7 days**

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

---

### Payment Methods (s.165)

Licensees **cannot**:
- Accept payment by credit card (including electronic/digital means funded by a credit card)
- Extend a credit facility to a participant
- Participate in, arrange, permit or facilitate the giving of credit in connection with a gambling activity
- Accept payment through e-wallets funded by credit cards

**Prohibited schemes include:** "buy now, pay later", betting tabs, and loans/credit lines from third-party finance companies.

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

---

### Remote Gambling — Account Requirements (ss.167–174)

#### Before Providing Remote Gambling (s.167)

A person may only participate in remote gambling if they hold an account. Licensees must confirm that:
1. The person has opened a gambling account
2. The person's name, address and date of birth have been recorded in the register of account-holders
3. The person has received the licensee's terms and conditions in writing

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

#### Register of Account-holders (s.168)

Remote licensees must maintain a register of account-holders containing:
- Name, address and date of birth of every account-holder
- Records of how terms and conditions and information obligations have been fulfilled
- Any other information required by GRAI regulations

Accounts must be **removed from the register** when closed (at the account-holder's request or after **13 months of inactivity**).

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

#### Opening a Remote Gambling Account (s.169)

Applicants must be **aged 18 or over**. Licensees must verify identity and age by requiring one of the following:

- A document specifying name, address and date of birth with a photo (e.g. driving licence), **or**
- A photo ID document specifying name and date of birth (e.g. passport) **plus** two documents confirming address (e.g. utility bill and insurance policy)

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

#### Closing a Remote Gambling Account (s.171)

Accounts must be closed:
- On written request from the account-holder, or
- After **13 months of inactivity** (excluding periods on the NER)

The licensee must refund any money in the account. If unable to refund after reasonable efforts (inactive account), the money must be transferred to the GRAI for the Social Impact Fund.

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

#### Terms and Conditions (s.172)

Remote licensees must:
- Provide terms and conditions when a person accesses remote gambling for the first time, and each time the terms change
- Ensure terms and conditions are accessible on their website at all times
- Write terms in **clear and plain language**
- Require express acceptance before payment is accepted
- Provide terms in a format the account-holder can store and retrieve

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

#### Information to Account-holders (s.173)

The GRAI will prescribe regulations on information to be provided relating to:
- Recognition of excessive or compulsive gambling and its adverse effects
- Facilities to support responsible gambling
- Self-exclusion tools (including blocking access for children)
- Gambling support services

This information must be **prominently displayed** on the licensee's homepage and each online platform.

Licensees must also:
- Send periodic alerts to account-holders detailing winnings, losses and time spent gambling
- Provide immediate account access to balance and transaction history

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

#### Protection of Children (s.174)

Remote licensees must display a **hyperlink to parental control programmes** on their homepage and all online platforms where gambling is provided.

---

### National Gambling Exclusion Register (NER) (ss.45–46)

The GRAI will establish a National Gambling Exclusion Register allowing persons to self-exclude from remote gambling activities.

Remote gambling licensees must:
- View NER information in **real time**
- Once a person is entered on the NER, **not** provide remote gambling to that person, accept payments from them, or communicate with them in a way that invites participation
- **Refund any money** in the person's account within **7 days** of notification

Exclusion takes effect **no earlier than one day** after the person's information is entered on the NER.

If a licensee cannot view the NER in real time (for reasons other than a NER systems failure), they must not provide any of the above services during that period.

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

---

### In-person Premises Obligations

#### Opening Hours (s.176)

The GRAI may prescribe days or hours during which in-person gambling premises cannot open. The GRAI may also prohibit the sale of items on premises that would entice gambling. Regulations cannot affect opening hours of betting offices at greyhound racetracks or authorised racecourses.

#### Children on Premises (s.177)

In-person licensees must not permit persons under 18 to enter gambling premises unless the licence specifically authorises children on premises. Even where children are permitted, they must not be allowed to participate in gambling activities.

**Reasonable mistake as to age may be a defence.**

#### ATMs on Premises (s.178)

Licensees must not permit ATMs on their premises unless their licence specifically authorises them to do so.

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

## Enforcement and Penalties

If a licensee breaches an obligation under the Act, the GRAI may take the following action:

### Criminal Prosecution

| Proceeding | Potential Penalty |
|---|---|
| Summary prosecution | Fine of up to **€5,000**, imprisonment up to **12 months**, or both |
| Conviction on indictment (lower-tier offence) | Fine and/or imprisonment up to **5 years** |
| Conviction on indictment (higher-tier offence) | Fine and/or imprisonment up to **8 years** |

### Civil / Regulatory Enforcement

- **Notice of non-compliance** — requiring the licensee to comply
- **Imposition of additional licence conditions**
- **Suspension or revocation of licence**
- **Financial penalty** — up to **€20,000,000 or 10% of turnover** (whichever is greater)

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **GRAI** | Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland (Údarás Rialála Cearrbhachais na hÉireann) |
| **B2C Licensee** | A Business-to-Consumer gambling licensee |
| **B2B Licensee** | A Business-to-Business gambling licensee |
| **Relevant Officer** | A director, manager, secretary, board member, CEO, or person exercising control in relation to a body corporate (or equivalent for non-Irish entities) |
| **Beneficial Owner** | As defined in Article 3 of EU Directive 2015/849 (Anti-Money Laundering Directive) |
| **Turnover** | For B2B licensees: gross profit on the sale/supply of gambling products or services. For remote betting intermediary licensees: total charges received. For all other licensees: total income from gambling activities less total winnings paid out |
| **Winnings** | Money or money's worth paid out to a person who wins at betting, wins a game or wins a lottery |
| **Relevant Offence** | An offence set out in Schedule 1 of the Gambling Regulation Act 2024 |
| **Suspicious Gambling Pattern** | A pattern suggesting an attempt to influence the outcome of a gambling activity |
| **Segregated Customer Account** | A single account held with a regulated financial service provider to hold customer funds separate from the licensee's own funds |
| **NER** | National Gambling Exclusion Register |
| **Social Impact Fund** | Fund established under the Act to support gambling harm reduction and research |
| **Relevant Premises** | As defined in Part 5 of the Gambling Regulation Act 2024 |
