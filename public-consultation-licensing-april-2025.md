## About Us

The Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland (GRAI) was established on **5 March 2025**. Its role is to regulate gambling activities in Ireland under the Gambling Regulation Act 2024 (referred to as "the 2024 Act").

Those who wish to provide gambling activities in Ireland require a licence from the GRAI. The GRAI can issue different types of gambling licences for in-person or remote gambling activities, including:

1. **Business to Consumer (B2C) gambling licences** — for those providing betting, gaming or lottery activities to consumers.
2. **Business to Business (B2B) gambling licences** — for those selling or supplying a gambling product or gambling-related service.
3. **Licences for a charitable and philanthropic purpose** — for those providing betting, gaming or lottery activities for charitable and philanthropic purposes.

The GRAI intends to open licensing in a phased way, with different licence category types opening at different times:

- **Business to Consumer betting licences** (both in-person and remote): target opening December 2025
- **Remote gaming licence applications**: towards the end of Q1 2026
- Order and timing may be subject to change

The licence application process takes a number of months. Once submitted, applications are reviewed to ensure all necessary information and documentation has been provided. The GRAI then undertakes various checks on the applicant and the information submitted.

## What is the Purpose of this Consultation?

The GRAI has the power to make regulations under the 2024 Act. This consultation sought feedback on two sets of proposed regulations:

### First Set of Regulations — Application Fees

Sets the application fee for betting (in-person and remote) and gaming (in-person and remote) licences, as provided for under section 38 of the 2024 Act.

### Second Set of Regulations — Licence Duration and Additional Conditions

Sets:
- The duration for which a gambling licence remains in force (s.108 of the 2024 Act)
- Additional conditions attaching to gambling licences (s.129 of the 2024 Act)

## About the Regulations

### (a) Application Fees

Application fees are **non-refundable** and must be paid in full at the time of applying. Applicants can apply for more than one licence, with a separate fee payable for each.

Fees are proposed on a **tiered fixed fee basis** by reference to turnover and licence type.

> **Note:** The application fee covers only the cost of submitting a licence application. If a licence is granted, licensees will also be required to pay:
> - A separate annual **charge to fund GRAI operational costs** (subject to a future consultation)
> - An annual **contribution to the Social Impact Fund** (subject to a future consultation)

#### How to Calculate Turnover for Fee Purposes

- **Remote betting intermediary licence:** total amount paid to the applicant for providing the facility that enables a person to engage in betting.
- **All other betting and gaming licences:** total income from betting and gaming activities less the total amount paid out in winnings.

Turnover is calculated on the basis of the applicant's **last financial year** before the date of the application. For new operators with no prior Ireland activity, use the **estimated turnover for the first 12 months** following the grant of the licence.

#### Notes on the Fee Tables

1. **Table A of Schedule 1** — In-person betting licences where betting activities are to be provided from a **relevant premises** (i.e. on-course bookmaking).
2. **Table B of Schedule 1** — In-person betting licences where betting activities are to be provided from **other premises** (i.e. off-course bookmaking such as a betting shop).
3. If betting is to be provided from both on-course and off-course locations, the applicant pays **both** the Table A and Table B fees (to be added together).
4. **Table D of Schedule 1** — Remote betting intermediary licence fee. A remote betting intermediary provides a facility to enable another person to engage in remote betting with others who are not the intermediary.
5. A **gaming licence** enables the licensee to provide one or more games (e.g. slots, roulette, blackjack), either in-person or remotely. The games permitted will be specified on the licence.

### (b) Licence Duration and Additional Conditions

#### Licence Duration

Under section 108(4) of the 2024 Act, the GRAI may set the period during which a B2C gambling licence remains in force.

The GRAI initially intends to set a **3-year duration** for all B2C gambling licences. This supports the phased approach to opening licensing — ensuring all B2C licence types are open before renewal applications need to be considered.

#### Additional Conditions for Remote B2C Licensees

The GRAI intends to set additional conditions for remote B2C licensees. At the time initial licences are granted, certain standard checks will **not** yet be carried out, because relevant standards under section 123 of the 2024 Act and the National Gambling Exclusion Register will not yet be operational. These deferred checks include:

- Whether software manufactured or adapted by the licensee meets relevant standards
- Whether software obtained from a third party is from a B2B licensee
- Whether gambling-related services meet standards in effect at the time
- Whether the software enables the licensee to view, in real time, information on the National Gambling Exclusion Register

The additional conditions will ensure that once standards take effect and the NER is established, existing licensees must provide the necessary information and documentation to allow these checks to be carried out.
