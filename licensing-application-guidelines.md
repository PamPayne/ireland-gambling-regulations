## Introduction

The Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland (GRAI / Údarás Rialála Cearrbhachais na hÉireann) was established on **5 March 2025** under the Gambling Regulation Act 2024. Its role is to license and regulate gambling providers in Ireland.

This document guides applicants seeking a gambling licence under the new and interim licensing arrangements.

---

## Licensing and Regulation

Under the Gambling Regulation Act 2024, providers of gambling activities operating in Ireland or providing gambling products/services to consumers in Ireland require a **gambling licence granted by the GRAI**.

The initial focus is on opening for **Business-to-Consumer (B2C) licence applications**. Applications for B2B licences and licences for charitable and philanthropic purposes will be accepted at a later stage.

Online applications for both in-person and remote B2C betting licences will be accepted. See the GRAI website for details on phased opening of licence categories.

---

## Timelines

The GRAI is adopting a phased approach to opening licensing:

- **B2C Betting licences** (in-person and remote): first to open
- **Gaming and Lottery licences**: to follow
- **B2B licences** and **charitable/philanthropic licences**: later stage

In the interim, existing and prospective operators are encouraged to apply for and renew licensing under existing arrangements with the **Revenue Commissioners** under the Betting Act 1931 and the Gaming and Lotteries Act 1956.

> Note: Revenue Commissioners will continue issuing betting licences in 2025 to facilitate the smooth transition of licensing regimes, but these licences will only be valid for a **one-year term**.

Lottery licence and permit applications continue to be assessed by the District Courts and An Garda Síochána respectively until new licensing arrangements are in place.

---

## Exemptions

Certain types of lotteries are exempt from licensing requirements. Visit the GRAI website for details of the criteria which must be satisfied for exemption: [www.grai.ie](https://www.grai.ie).

## Application Process Overview

### Step 1 — Publish Notice of Intention

Publish your Notice of Intention to apply for a gambling licence **at least 28 days before** submitting your application. Provide the GRAI with a copy of the Notice of Intention including proof of publication. A dedicated mailing address will be supplied when applicable, along with additional guidance on publication.

### Step 2 — Document Preparation

Ensure all necessary documentation is ready for submission (see [Section-by-Section Guide](#section-by-section-guide-to-completing-an-application) below). All submitted documents will be treated with appropriate care and diligence.

### Step 3 — Submission

Complete the application process online, ensuring all information is accurate and truthful. The **full, non-refundable application fee** must be paid at time of submission. Fees are set on a tiered basis, commensurate with the applicant's turnover in the preceding year of operation (or projected turnover in the first year of trading for newly established operators).

### Step 4 — Review

The GRAI will review licence applications and perform a **risk-based assessment** of the applicant's suitability to provide gambling activities. Processing times may vary depending on complexity. Additional information may be requested. The GRAI may also request or exchange information with other regulatory bodies and authorities as part of due diligence checks on the suitability of the applicant and its officers and owners.

### Step 5 — Assessment of Suitability of Premises (In-person Licences)

For in-person licence applications, the GRAI will arrange a **physical attendance** by a GRAI representative at the premises to confirm suitability. Required supporting documentation includes:

- Maps of the premises
- Planning Permission documentation
- Opinion/Certificates of Compliance with Building Regulations
- Planning and Fire Safety documentation

### Step 6 — Decision

The GRAI will assess all documentation and consider all relevant information before determining suitability for licensing. Applicants will be **notified in writing** of the decision to grant or refuse a licence.

### Step 7 — Post-Licence Approval

Licensees must notify the GRAI of any material changes or matters of concern, including but not limited to:

- Changes in ownership or control
- Changes in financial circumstances (which may necessitate a variation of licence application if likely to exceed the application fee category limit)
- Conviction of the licensee, a relevant officer, or a beneficial owner for a relevant offence

## Section-by-Section Guide to Completing an Application

### Section 1 — Business Registration

This section requires applicants to demonstrate they are of **sound financial standing** and adhere to **strong governance practices**. These licensing principles must continue to be satisfied for the duration the licence is in force. Failure to satisfy any requirement can lead to the **revocation or suspension** of the gambling licence.

**Required information and supporting documentation:**

**Business Information**
- Trading name
- Registered business address
- Tax clearance information

**Corporate Documentation**
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Constitution documents
- Shareholders Register

**Relevant Policies and Procedures**
- Anti-Money Laundering (AML) / Counter-Terrorism Financing (CTF) policy
- Know Your Customer (KYC) policy
- Responsible Gambling policy
- Risk Management policy
- Whistleblower Policy
- Business Continuity Plans

**Financial Information**
- Financial Statements
- Bank Statements
- Loan agreements
- Business Plan with Financial Projections

**Other Required Information**
- Details of any other gambling or non-gambling licences held or previously held (Ireland or overseas)
- Details of the applicant's beneficial owners and relevant officers
- Details of any convictions, criminal proceedings or regulatory action currently initiated (and not yet concluded) or previously taken against the applicant, its relevant officers or beneficial owners for relevant offences

All information provided must be accurate and all questions must be answered.

---

### Section 1b — Account Officer Registration

If making the application on behalf of a corporate body, details of **all beneficial owners and relevant officers** must be provided. Each listed officer and owner will receive an email asking them to complete a separate application form providing:

- Proof of identity and address
- Details of any gambling or non-gambling licences held or previously held (Ireland or overseas)
- Details of any criminal convictions for a relevant offence, proceedings or sanctions against the individual
- Tax clearance information

---

### Section 2 — Licence Application

Confirm the licence type being applied for. Applicants must complete this section for **each licence** they wish to obtain.

#### Licence Types

| Licence | Description |
|---|---|
| **In-person Betting Licence** | Authorises provision of betting activities from one or more premises in Ireland, or pool betting for an event, series of events or class of events |
| **Remote Betting Licence** | Authorises provision of betting activities by remote means, including pool betting by remote means |
| **Remote Betting Intermediary Licence** | Authorises provision of a facility enabling individuals to bet with other individuals (not the intermediary) by remote means |

> Gaming licences and Lottery licences will be opened for applications in due course.

#### Additional Information Required by Licence Type

- **In-person betting:** channel(s) — on course, off course, or both; event details (pool betting); premises locations including maps, floor plans, deeds/lease arrangements, whether children will be permitted, whether ATMs will be provided
- **Remote gambling:** technical and software information; customer terms and conditions

The **full application fee** must be paid alongside submission of the licence application.

## Glossary of Terms

### Relevant Officer

As defined in the Gambling Regulation Act 2024, a "relevant officer" means:

**(a) In relation to a body corporate:**
- A person who exercises control (within the meaning of section 11 or 432 of the Taxes Consolidation Act 1997) in relation to the body
- A director, manager, secretary or other officer of the body
- A member of the board (including the chairperson)
- The chief executive officer, managing director or any other person acting in such capacity
- A person who purports to act in any of the above capacities

**(b) In relation to a body corporate not established in the State:**
- A person who holds an office or position equivalent to any of the above
- A person who purports to act in such a capacity

### Beneficial Owner

Has the meaning given by Article 3 of Directive (EU) 2015/849 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 20 May 2015 on the prevention of the use of the financial system for the purposes of money laundering or terrorist financing.

### Relevant Offence

An offence set out in **Schedule 1** of the Gambling Regulation Act 2024.

### Turnover

As defined in the Act:
- **B2B licensee:** gross profit on the sale or supply of relevant gambling products or relevant gambling-related services
- **Remote betting intermediary licensee:** total amount paid in charges to the licensee for providing the facility enabling a person to engage in betting
- **All other licensees:** total income from relevant gambling activities less the total amount paid out in winnings for those activities

**Winnings** means the money or money's worth paid out to a person who wins at betting, wins a game, or wins in a lottery.
