## Purpose of Guidance

This document provides practical guidance to **Business to Consumer (B2C) licensees** on the application of conditions and obligations set out in the Gambling Regulation Act 2024 and regulations made under the Act. It does not address obligations arising outside of the Act.

---

## Who Do These Obligations Apply To?

These conditions and obligations apply to **B2C licensees**.

---

## What Are Conditions and Obligations?

**Conditions** are requirements attaching to gambling licences by virtue of particular provisions of the Act or by virtue of regulations made under section 129. Further conditions may be imposed as a result of enforcement action.

**Obligations** include any other requirement imposed on licensees under the Act or in regulations.

Collectively, these are referred to in the Act as **"relevant obligations"**.

---

## Consequences of Breach

If a licensee breaches an obligation, the GRAI can deal with this in different ways:

### Criminal Prosecution

| Proceeding | Potential Penalty |
|---|---|
| Summary prosecution | Fine up to **€5,000**, imprisonment up to **12 months**, or both |
| Conviction on indictment | Fine and/or imprisonment up to **5 or 8 years** (depending on the offence) |

### Enforcement Action (Non-criminal)

- Issuing a **notice of non-compliance** requiring the licensee to comply
- **Imposing conditions** on a licence
- **Suspension or revocation** of a licence
- **Financial penalty** of up to **€20,000,000 or 10% of turnover** (whichever is greater)

---

## When Will Conditions and Obligations Take Effect?

The GRAI is adopting a phased approach to opening licensing. The majority of conditions and obligations take effect when the GRAI opens for licensing. Conditions and obligations only apply once an operator has been issued with a licence.

## Conditions and Obligations in Detail

---

### s.45 — National Gambling Exclusion Register

The Authority will establish a **National Gambling Exclusion Register (NER)** containing information on persons who have excluded themselves from remote gambling activities.

Remote gambling licensees must be able to **view the NER in real time** once it becomes operational. The Authority may require remote gambling licensees to take specified action to promote the NER.

Once notified that an account-holder has entered themselves on the NER, the licensee must **refund any money** in that person's account **within 7 days** of the specified date.

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply with the refund requirement.

---

### s.46 — Obligations of Licensee to Person on NER

If a person's information has been entered on the NER, a remote gambling licensee must **not**, once that person is excluded and during any time they remain on the NER:

- **(a)** Provide a remote gambling activity to that person
- **(b)** Accept a payment from that person in respect of a remote gambling activity
- **(c)** Communicate with the person in a way that invites (or has the effect of inviting) them to participate in a remote gambling activity

Exclusion takes effect **no earlier than one day** after the person's information is entered on the NER.

If the licensee cannot view the NER in real time (for reasons other than a NER systems failure), they must not do any of the above during the period they are unable to view the information.

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.67 — Prohibition on Providing Betting Activity, Game or Lottery

Licensees must not provide a betting activity, game or lottery **otherwise than in accordance with their licence**.

Licences specify:
- The relevant gambling activity the licensee can provide
- Events or class of events for pool betting
- Games a gaming licensee can provide
- The number of gaming machines a gaming licensee can use

**Practical Examples:**
- A licensee only licensed for in-person betting cannot provide remote betting
- A licensee only licensed for in-person gaming cannot provide remote gaming
- An in-person betting licensee licensed for pool betting for a series of events commencing 1 July and concluding 1 October cannot provide pool betting after 1 October
- An in-person gaming licensee licensed for 20 gaming machines cannot have more than 20 in use

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.69 — Prohibition on Betting Intermediaries Acting Other Than by Remote Means

A person can only provide intermediary services (a facility enabling another person to engage in betting with others who are not the intermediary) if they hold a **remote betting intermediary licence**.

There is no in-person betting intermediary licence under the Act.

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.74 — Cheating at a Relevant Gambling Activity

A person who **cheats** at a relevant gambling activity is guilty of a criminal offence. It is irrelevant whether the person makes a gain or improves their chances of making a gain.

Cheating includes interfering or manipulating:
- The process by which a relevant gambling activity is provided
- A relevant gambling product or service used for a relevant gambling activity
- An event to which a relevant gambling activity relates
- The outcome of a relevant gambling activity

**Examples of cheating:**
- Using manipulated gaming apparatus (rigged roulette wheels, loaded dice, non-standard card decks, false shuffling)
- Misstating the chances of winning to induce people to gamble under false pretences
- Exploiting software to pay out lower returns than advertised rates

---

### s.110 — Display and Production of Gambling Licence

Every licensee is issued a gambling licence with a **registration number**.

- **In-person licences:** must be displayed in a **prominent location** at each premises
- **Remote licences:** must be displayed in a **prominent location** on all online platforms

The registration number must be included in **all business correspondence and advertisements** relating to gambling activities.

Licensees must provide a copy of their licence to GRAI **authorised officers** on request.

**Practical Examples:**
- In-person betting licensees must display their licence at each betting premises (appendices need not be displayed but must be stored accessibly)
- Remote gaming licensees must display their licence on each website/app
- A licensee with both in-person and remote betting licences must display the in-person licence at each premises and the remote licence on each platform
- For licensees with remote betting and remote gaming licences, both must be displayed on each platform

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.129 — Regulations

The GRAI may make regulations under section 129 imposing **additional conditions** on B2C licences. This guidance will be updated to include these additional conditions as and when section 129 regulations are made.

---

### s.131 — Conditions Attaching to B2C Gambling Licences

A B2C gambling licence is granted subject to the following conditions:

- **(a)** Having the **financial capacity** to provide the relevant gambling activity and to fund winnings
- **(b)** Funding winnings from **lawful activities**
- **(c)** The licensee, relevant officers and beneficial owners continuing to be a **fit and proper person** to hold the licence
- **(d)** Providing only the **relevant gambling activity** authorised under the licence
- **(e)** Payments and winnings not exceeding **maximum limits** in Schedule 3 of the Act
- **(f)** Where maximum payment/winnings limits apply, displaying them **prominently** at each premises and on each online platform
- **(g)** Notifying the Authority of changes to information given under section 102 (software/ICT hardware), within **7 days** of a relevant change
- **(h)** If changes to section 102 information are notified, the Authority being satisfied the licensee can still view the NER in real time and the software remains compliant with section 123 standards
- **(i)** Paying the **annual charge** to fund GRAI operational costs within 30 days of notice

---

### s.132 — Notice to Authority of Change of Information Given under Section 98(1)

In-person gambling licensees must notify the GRAI of any changes to premises information (as specified by the Authority by notice on its website) **within 7 days** of a relevant change.

---

### s.133 — Notice to Authority of Change in Information Given under Section 102

Remote gambling licensees must notify the GRAI of changes to the following, within **7 days** of a relevant change:
1. The location and type of ICT hardware
2. The software used to facilitate real-time access to the NER
3. Once section 123 standards are in effect: information about the software and whether it was manufactured, installed, maintained or upgraded by the licensee or a third party

---

### s.134 — Additional Conditions: Business to Consumer Gambling Licences

B2C gambling licences are also subject to:

- **(a)** Establishing and maintaining a **Segregated Customer Account** (except once-off lottery licensees)
- **(b)** Where children are permitted on premises, complying with any conditions imposed by the Authority under section 100(3)
- **(c)** Complying with GRAI determinations on the number and location of **ATMs** permitted on premises
- **(d)** Complying with section 132 (notice of change to premises information)
- **(e)** Where the Authority receives notice under section 132, the Authority continuing to be satisfied the premises remains appropriate for the gambling activity

**Example:** An in-person gaming licensee who is found to have more ATMs than permitted will be in breach.

---

### s.135 — Establishment and Maintenance of Segregated Customer Account

Licensees must open and maintain a **single account** with a regulated financial service provider to hold customer funds (the "Segregated Customer Account").

**The account must hold:**
- Money held by account-holders and in-person participants
- Payments made in connection with gambling activities not yet completed
- Unpaid winnings

**Licensees may only access the account:**
- When given permission by the account-holder or in-person participant
- When the gambling activity has been completed
- To pay out winnings

Licensees must maintain up-to-date records enabling identification of:
- The amount standing to the credit of each account-holder/participant
- The total amount of payments held
- The amount of unpaid winnings

The GRAI may make further regulations under section 136 on Segregated Customer Accounts.

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

---

### s.138 — Additional Conditions: Lottery Licences

Lottery licensees must ensure every **lottery ticket** contains:
- The name and address of the licensee
- Any other information prescribed by the Authority in regulations

Where the lottery licence is for a **once-off lottery**, the lottery must be held on the date provided to the Authority in the application.

---

### s.142 — Not to Engage a Child as Employee

A licensee must not **engage or employ** (or cause to be engaged or employed) a **child** (person under 18) to provide gambling activities.

---

### s.152 — Obligation on Licensees to Maintain Records and Accounts

The GRAI will make regulations setting out the records and accounts that a licensee must keep (in addition to Segregated Customer Account records). Licensees will be notified in advance.

---

### s.153 — Obligation to Notify Authority of Changes

Licensees must notify the Authority in writing **within 7 days** of a **material event**:

1. A change in the **relevant officers or beneficial owners** of the licensee
2. A change to the licensee's **address** for receipt of notices under the Act
3. The licensee, a relevant officer or beneficial owner is **convicted** of a Schedule 1 offence, or proceedings are commenced
4. A finding, or proceedings taken against the licensee or any beneficial owner or relevant officer by a **regulatory authority, court or tribunal outside Ireland** relating to gambling obligations, or a sanction imposed
5. A change in the licensee's **financial circumstances** that impacts the capacity to provide gambling activities, fund winnings, or comply with the matters identified in this guidance

---

### s.155 — Obligation to Pay Out Winnings

A licensee must **not unreasonably withhold** the payment of winnings.

Winnings may be reasonably withheld where:
- The licensee becomes aware of a **suspicious gambling pattern** in relation to the gambling activity
- The participant has breached **reasonable contractual terms and conditions**

It is considered best practice to have an internal complaints/disputes process in place.

---

### s.156 — Obligation to Notify Authority of Suspicious Gambling Patterns

If a licensee becomes aware of a **suspicious gambling pattern** (an attempt to influence the outcome of a relevant gambling activity), they must:

- **(a)** **Stop accepting payments** in relation to the gambling activity
- **(b)** Issue a **written note** to each person attempting to make a payment stating the licensee will no longer accept payments in relation to that gambling activity, and keep copies
- **(c)** **Notify the Authority as soon as possible** about the suspicious gambling pattern, confirming payments are no longer being accepted
- **(d)** Provide the Authority with a copy of the note issued under (b) and any other records relating to the suspicious pattern
- **(e)** **Inform An Garda Síochána** as soon as possible
- **(f)** **Not pay out winnings** on the relevant gambling activity until the Authority confirms they may do so

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply with (a), (b), (c), (e) and (f).

**Examples of suspicious gambling patterns:**
- Bets placed on events where the integrity of that event is later called into question by an official body
- Bets placed by individuals who are involved in and could influence the outcome of an event
- A large and sudden volume of bets on a particular outcome, especially on obscure markets that typically have low volumes of activity

---

### s.157 — Offering Inducement to Gamble

An **inducement** (e.g. free bets, money-back guarantees) is a benefit or advantage intended directly or indirectly to encourage participation in gambling.

A licensee can only offer inducements to the **general public**. They **cannot** be offered to:
- A specific person
- Specific groups of persons
- Customers selected based on loyalty or betting volume

Further regulations regarding permissible inducements to the general public may be introduced in the future.

**Criminal offence** to offer inducements in breach of these restrictions.

**Examples of prohibited inducements:**
- Selecting loyal customers and offering them free bets
- Operating a reward or loyalty scheme for customers who bet a certain volume over a period of time

---

### s.158 — Not to Permit a Child to Participate in Gambling Activities

A licensee must not **permit a child to participate** in gambling activities.

**For in-person licensees:** if there is any doubt about whether a participant is 18, seek appropriate photo ID (e.g. passport, driving licence) containing name, date of birth and photo before allowing participation.

**For remote licensees:** all participants must open an account before engaging in gambling activities. Licensees must request appropriate identification and must be satisfied the person opening an account is over 18.

**Defence:** If a child is permitted to participate, a licensee may have a defence if they prove they were **reasonably mistaken** that the child was over 18.

---

### s.163 — Obligation to Ensure Approved Training Programme Completed by Staff

Licensees must ensure that all staff whose duties entail **engaging with participants** have completed an **GRAI-approved training programme** before they commence work, and at such intervals as the GRAI may require.

The GRAI will publish the content and standards for approved training programmes, and a list of approved programmes, on its website.

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.164 — Obligation to Provide Facility to Enable Participant to Set Monetary Limit

Licensees must enable participants to set a **spending limit** covering:
- **(a)** The amount they can pay to participate in any individual gambling activity
- **(b)** The total amount they can pay across all gambling activities provided by the licensee

Participants must be able to set the **time period** for the spending limit (no restrictions on length). Once set, participants **cannot remove or increase** the spending limit until after the time period expires.

**While the spending limit applies, licensees must:**
- Not provide a gambling activity that would cause the participant to exceed their limit
- If the limit is exceeded, **refund the excess within 7 days**
- Not communicate with the participant in a manner that invites or has the effect of inviting the participant to participate in a relevant gambling activity, or that advertises a relevant gambling activity to that participant

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

---

### s.165 — Obligation in Respect of Method of Payment

A licensee **cannot**:
- Accept payment by **credit card** (including electronic/digital means funded by a credit card)
- **Extend a credit facility** to a participant
- Participate in, arrange, permit or facilitate the giving of **credit** to a participant in connection with a gambling activity

Licensees should ensure that e-wallets used by customers are **not funded by credit cards**.

**Prohibited schemes include:** "buy now, pay later", betting tabs, loans or credit lines from third-party finance companies.

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.167 — Obligations Before Providing Gambling by Remote Means

A person may only participate in remote gambling if they hold an **account** with the licensee. Licensees must not provide remote gambling unless:

1. The person has **opened a gambling account** with the licensee
2. The licensee has **recorded** in their register of account-holders the person's name, address and date of birth, and how the obligations under sections 172 and 173 have been fulfilled
3. The person has **received the licensee's terms and conditions** in writing

The Authority may make regulations requiring a separate account for each remote gambling activity.

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

---

### s.168 — Obligation to Establish and Maintain a Register of Account-holders

Remote gambling licensees must maintain a **register of account-holders** containing:
1. Name, address and date of birth of every account-holder
2. How obligations under sections 172 (terms and conditions) and 173 (information) have been fulfilled
3. Any other information required by GRAI regulations

**Accounts must be removed** from the register when the gambling account is closed (at the account-holder's request or after **13 months of inactivity**, not counting periods when the account-holder was on the NER).

**Criminal offence** to fail to comply.

---

### s.169 — Application to Open Remote Gambling Account

Applicants must be **aged 18 or over**.

Licensees must **verify the identity and age** of all applicants by requiring:

**(Option a)** A document specifying name, address and date of birth with a photo of the person (e.g. driving licence)

**OR**

**(Option b)** A photo ID document specifying name and date of birth (e.g. passport) **plus** two documents confirming address (e.g. utility bill and insurance policy)

The licensee must be satisfied that the documents relate to the person applying and that they are over 18.

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.170 — Requirement to Lodge Money in a Gambling Account

The GRAI may make regulations:
- Limiting the amount a remote gambling licensee can require an account-holder to lodge
- Specifying the maximum amount an account-holder can lodge during a specified period

Licensees will be notified in advance and will have an opportunity to engage in consultation on draft regulations.

**Criminal offence** not to comply with these regulations once made.

---

### s.171 — Obligation to Close Gambling Account

A remote gambling licensee must **close an account** when:
- Requested in writing by the account-holder, or
- After **13 months of inactivity** (not counting periods when on the NER)

The licensee must **refund any money** in the account.

Where the account has been closed due to inactivity and the licensee cannot, after reasonable efforts, refund the money, the money must be **transferred to the Authority for payment into the Social Impact Fund**. If the account-holder subsequently contacts the licensee for the money, the licensee should direct them to the Authority and notify the Authority in writing.

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.172 — Obligation to Provide Copy of Terms and Conditions

Remote gambling licensees must provide their **terms and conditions**:
- When a person accesses remote gambling with that licensee for the **first time**
- On each occasion the **terms and conditions change** (when the person next accesses the service after the change)

Terms and conditions must:
- **(i)** Be written in **clear and plain language**
- **(ii)** Be **expressly accepted** by the account-holder before payment is accepted
- **(iii)** Be made available by **electronic means** so the account-holder can store and retrieve them

Terms and conditions must be **accessible at all times** on the licensee's website.

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.173 — Obligation to Provide Information to Account-holder

Remote gambling licensees must provide account-holders with information (to be specified in GRAI regulations) relating to:

- **(a)** Recognition of the identifying characteristics of **excessive or compulsive gambling** and its adverse effects
- **(b)** Facilities available to support **responsible gambling**
- **(c)** Facilities available to **block access** (for themselves or a child) to gambling websites
- **(d)** **Gambling support services**

This information must be:
- Provided to account-holders
- **Prominently displayed** on the licensee's homepage and on each online platform where gambling is accessible

Licensees must send **periodic alerts** to account-holders' accounts (at intervals set by the Authority) detailing:
- Winnings and losses
- Time spent participating in gambling activities through the account

Licensees must also provide account-holders with **immediate account access** to:
- The amount of money in their account
- Details of the amounts paid by the account-holder for each gambling activity over specified periods

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.174 — Protection of Children

Remote gambling licensees must display a **hyperlink to parental control programmes** on the homepage of their website and on each online platform where remote gambling is provided.

Parental control programmes help parents manage and restrict what their children can access on digital devices.

---

### s.175 — Obligation Not to Provide Gambling Activities by Remote Means on Certain Days or at Certain Times

The Authority can prescribe **days or hours** during which remote gambling licensees will be prevented from providing remote gambling activities.

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.176 — Obligations in Respect of Opening Hours and Use of Premises

The Authority can prescribe **days or hours** during which in-person gambling licensees cannot open their gambling premises.

> Regulations cannot affect the opening hours of betting offices at greyhound racetracks as permitted in regulations made by Rásaíocht Con Éireann, or at authorised racecourses as permitted by Horse Racing Ireland.

The Authority can also prohibit the sale by licensees of any matter on a gambling premises that it considers would entice a person to engage in gambling activities.

**Criminal offence** not to comply.

---

### s.177 — Children on Premises

An in-person licensee must not permit a person to enter a gambling premises unless satisfied they are **over 18**.

As an exception, if the licence permits it, children may be allowed on premises. The licensee must have made an application to the GRAI for children to be permitted. Even where permitted, it remains an offence to **permit a child to participate** in gambling activities.

If in doubt about a person's age, seek identification (e.g. passport, driver's licence) before allowing entry.

**Defence:** Reasonable mistake as to age — provided a reasonable person could have reached the same conclusion in the same circumstances.

---

### s.178 — Obligation in Relation to Withdrawal of Cash on Premises

Licensees must not permit **ATMs** on their premises unless their licence specifically authorises them to do so.

**Criminal offence** not to comply.
